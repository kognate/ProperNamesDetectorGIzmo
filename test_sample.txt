The Great Gatsby is a novel by F. Scott Fitzgerald, published in 1925.

The story is set in Long Island, New York during the Jazz Age. The main characters include Jay Gatsby, Nick Carraway, and Daisy Buchanan. The novel is considered one of the greatest American literary works.

In the story, Gatsby tries to win back Daisy, who is married to Tom Buchanan. The tragedy unfolds in West Egg, a fictional beach community inspired by real locations in New York.

The novel has been adapted multiple times, including a famous film version directed by Jack Clayton in 1974, and another by Baz Luhrmann in 2013.

Fitzgerald's masterpiece explores themes of wealth, love, and the American Dream during the Roaring Twenties.
